Instructions for making this product

1. Aquire a handheld fan.

2. Open the fan and take electronics out.

3. Cut the top of the fan case (the part where the motor was before you took it out).

4. Solder wires on electronics back together if it fell apart.

5. Put the electronics and the case back together so the motor is dangling outside from where the cut was made.

6. 3D print the new fan tip (a hollow cube with an open side and a hole big enough for the axle (the spinning part) in the middle of the opposite side).

7. Print out a screw with a hole through the middle about the size of the axle.

8. Glue the screw onto the axle.

9. Print/create the different motor tips with a base that can be screwed onto the screw on the motor.

10. Print out the big square base with a hole in each corner for the product.

11. Print out the piece that goes under the fan case (a smaller square that covers the base of the fan case and also has a hole in each corner) then glue it onto the bottom of the fan case.

12. Aquire two pairs of chopsticks (four sticks total) and connect to the base and the fan by putting one end of each stick into each hole, then putting the other end in the respecting holes on the fan.
